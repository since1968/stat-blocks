Arbalester

Medium humanoid (any race), any alignment

Armor Class

15 (chain shirt) / 20 with pavise

Hit Points

32 (5d8 + 10)

Speed

30 ft.

Skills

Athletics +2, Survival +2. Optional: Animal Handling +2

Senses

passive Perception 10

Languages

any one language (usually Common)

Challenge

2 (450 XP)

Pavise.

The arbalester may use an action to place his pavise in position. The arbalester then fires with three-quarters cover (+5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws). While protected by the pavise, the arblester's movement rate is 0. The arbalester must use one action to stow the shield. The pavise may not be wielded in hand to hand combat.

Unit Tactics.

The arbalester has advantage on a ranged attack roll against a creature if at least one of the arbalester's allies is within 5 feet of the arbalester and isn't incapacitated.

Actions

Shortsword.

Melee Weapon Attack: +2 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 6 (1d6 + 2) piercing damage.

Dagger.

Melee Weapon Attack: +5 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. Hit: 5 (1d4 + 3) piercing damage.

Heavy Crossbow.

Ranged Weapon Attack: +3 to hit, range 100/400 ft., one target. Hit: 8 (1d10 + 3) piercing damage.

Deriving their advantage by fighting as a unit, Arbalesters are professional fighers trained to use crossbows in group formation and do not excel as individual fighters. Arbalesters will take up arms for pay or to protect something they believe in or value. Unless overrun, they will not engage in melee combat.

Variant: Mounted Infantry. Arbalesters with animal handling skill may be used as mounted infantry but do not fight on horseback.
