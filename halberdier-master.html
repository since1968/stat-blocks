Unit Tactics.

The Master Halberdier has advantage on a melee attack roll against a creature if at least one of the Master Halberdier's allies is within 5 feet of the Master Halberdier and isn't incapacitated.

Polearm Master.

When the Master Halberdier is wielding a halberd, other creatures provoke an opportunity attack when entering the Master Hablerdier's reach. The Master Hablerdier can also use a bonus action to attack with the opposite end of the halberd. This attack does 1d4 bludgeoning damage.

Sentinel.

When the Master Halberdier hits a creature with an opportunity attack, the creature's speed becomes zero for the rest of its turn. Creatures provoke opportunity attacks even if they take the Disengage action.

Unseat Dat Horseman.

The Master Halberdier may use the ready action to defend against a cavalry charge to attempt to unseat the charging horseman. As the horseman attacks, he must roll a Strength saving throw (DC 15) or be pulled from his mount. The unseated horseman takes 1d6 points of damage and is knocked prone. The unseating action must be taken in lieu of an attack.

Deriving their advantage by fighting as a unit, Master Halberdiers are professional fighers trained to use halberds in group formation and do not excel as individual fighters. Halberdiers will take up arms for pay or to protect something they believe in or value. They trained to take down cavalry and are especially adept at stopping an enemy charge.

Variant: Mounted Infantry. Halberdiers with animal handling skill may be used as mounted infantry but do not fight on horseback.
